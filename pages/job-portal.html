The Concept

Two very different platforms, one experience.

Most job hunting today happens across two disconnected kinds of apps. There's the professional network, like LinkedIn, where visibility and profile activity matter, and there's the plain job board, where you scroll listings and apply into a void. Neither one really talks to the other.

Job Portal was designed to sit in between the two, a recruiter and job seeker platform in one, that keeps the social, visible feel of a network while staying focused on the one thing people actually opened the app for: finding or filling a role.

Design Highlights

What makes it feel less like a black box.

The design leans on a few specific decisions to bridge that gap between "networking app" and "job board."

01
Dual-sided experience
Recruiters and seekers share one platform instead of two separate apps, with each side getting a view tailored to what they're actually there to do.
02
Profile visits
Borrowed straight from LinkedIn's playbook. Seeing who viewed your profile turns job hunting from a silent void into something that feels a little more human.
03
Jobs for Women
A dedicated section surfacing roles and employers specifically welcoming to women, so that search doesn't get buried in a general feed.
04
Focused discovery
Underneath the social layer, the core job discovery and application flow stays simple and uncluttered, so the networking features add to it instead of getting in the way.
