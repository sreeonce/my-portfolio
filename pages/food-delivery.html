Food Delivery App

A food ordering app built around one question most delivery apps never ask: why should one order have to come from just one restaurant?

The Concept

Why limit an order to one restaurant?

Almost every food delivery app works the same way. You pick a restaurant, order from its menu, and if you want something from somewhere else, that's a whole separate order, a separate delivery fee, a separate wait. It's a limitation nobody really questions because it's just how the category works.

This app questions it anyway. It's built so a single cart, and a single order, can pull dishes from as many restaurants as you want, alongside a browsing experience that's more fun to scroll through than the usual restaurant-list grind.

Design Highlights

What makes ordering more flexible.

A few specific decisions set this apart from a typical single-restaurant ordering flow.

01
Order across restaurants
Add dishes from multiple restaurants into one cart and check out once, instead of placing separate orders for every place you want food from.
02
Veg / non-veg toggle
A single switch filters the entire browsing experience, so users don't have to sift through options that don't fit their diet.
03
Browse by dish or cuisine
Discovery isn't locked to restaurant names. Users can browse by the actual dish or cuisine they're craving instead.
04
Fun order history
Past orders show up as a proper receipt-style layout, playful enough that checking your order history doesn't feel like reading a spreadsheet.
