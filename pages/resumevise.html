The Concept

Free shouldn't mean generic.

Most resume builders either charge for anything actually useful or hand you a blank template and call it done, with no guidance on what to actually put in it. Students and early-career job seekers, the people who need help the most, end up with the least support.

ResumeVise was designed to be genuinely free and genuinely helpful. Enter your experience and skills, pick a resume or cover letter template, and let the design generate a solid first draft, with tips along the way instead of an empty page and good luck.

Design Highlights

More than just a template picker.

The design is built around getting someone from "I have no idea what to write" to a finished draft, then giving them somewhere to go next.

01
Simple detail intake
A guided flow for entering experience, skills, and background, so users aren't staring at a blank form wondering where to start.
02
Template selection
A choice of resume and cover letter templates, so the final draft looks intentional rather than like everyone else's.
03
Tips and guides
Contextual guidance woven into the process itself, not buried in a separate help page nobody opens.
04
Explore hiring resources
A dedicated space pointing users toward where to actually get hired, from major MNC hiring pages to other resources worth checking out.
